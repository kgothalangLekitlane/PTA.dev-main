Explore Pretoria

The Union Buildings

The Union Buildings, completed in 1913, are a key landmark in Pretoria. Designed by Sir Herbert Baker, this grand structure serves as the seat of the South African government and symbolizes national unity. The buildings are surrounded by beautifully manicured gardens and offer panoramic views of the city. They also host important state functions and ceremonies.

Pretoria Botanical Gardens

Established in 1946, the Pretoria National Botanical Garden is renowned for its extensive collection of indigenous plants. Spanning over 75 hectares, the garden is a tranquil haven for nature enthusiasts and researchers alike. It features diverse plant collections, scenic walking trails, and a peaceful setting perfect for relaxation and exploration.

Freedom Park

Freedom Park is a national heritage site dedicated to the struggle for freedom in South Africa. Opened in 2007, it is a place of reflection and remembrance, honoring those who fought for democracy and human rights. The park includes memorials, museums, and gardens, providing a poignant experience of South Africa's journey to freedom.
